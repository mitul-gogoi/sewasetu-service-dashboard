Sewa Setu Service Delivery Performance Dashboard

Dashboard Overview

The Sewa Setu Service Delivery Performance Dashboard provides comprehensive insights into online service delivery across various departments via the Sewa Setu platform. This interactive dashboard enables departmental users and senior stakeholders to track, assess, and manage the timeliness and efficiency of public service delivery.

Dashboard Components

The dashboard is organized into the following key sections:

1. Key Metrics:

At the top of the dashboard, users can quickly view high-level statistics:

  • Total Applications received: Based on the filter conditions applied.
  • Total Delivered: Cases where service has been delivered.
  • Total Rejected: Cases rejected.
  • Total Returned: Cases returned back to the applicant.
  • Currently Open applications: Cases where processing is ongoing as on date.
  • Applications Overdue: Cases where the time since request for application has exceeded the timeline committed for the service, be it RTPS, or not.

2. Filters for Analysis:

While the Dashboard itself is interactive, there are a few high level filters Interactive filters that can be applied to focus on the services of interest:

  • RTPS Notified: Filter applications notified under the Right to Public Services Act.
  • Service Category: Enables filtering for services based on their intended beneficiaries/categories: Business, Citizens, Schemes, Utility.
  • o Select Month (From and To):Define a time range for viewing the status of only those services that were applied for in that time period.

3. Department-wise Performance Table:

This section provides an overview of Service delivery performance by department, with information on the number of Services being delivered through Sewa Setu, the number of applications, the percentage share of the applications, and the count of Open and overdue cases. One or more Departments can be selected using the drop-box, or by clicking on the name of the Department to focus on their performance.

4. Service-wise Performance Table:

This shows similar summary information as shown in case of Departments, allowing ranking and sorting of the Services by any of the numbers, viz Number of applications, Number Open, and Number Overdue. Additionally, if a Department had been selected earlier, one can focus on the services being rendered by that Department alone.

5. The above sections provide both a summary, and an option to Filter and focus on the performance of the selected Department and/or Service for a particular period. Subsequent section shows other data relating to the performance.

6. Service Delivery Speed (Past Performance):

This enables a quick assessment of what has been the historical performance of the selected service(s) in terms of the time taken in delivering services. This considers only those cases where the services were actually delivered (excludes returned/rejected/open cases)

7. Current Pendency of Open Applications:

While the past performance is seen in the previous visualisation focusing on historical, the current pendency visualisation enables in understanding what the extent of Pendency is, and how delayed are the cases. This is especially crucially for RTPS services where strict timelines have been committed by the Government, and enforceable through the RTPS Act.

8. Trend of Application Receipts:

The chart shows the monthly trends of application receipt. This helps in identifying peak periods, seasonal trends, and a possible explanation for the current status of pendency.

9. Districts & Offices Performance:

The District and Office level performance Tabulations summarises open and overdue applications at district and office level. This facilitates localized performance management and identifying Geographical location / offices that need attention to meet the performance targets.

How to Use the Dashboard

10. The following is one possible approach for using the Dashboard, though users may evolve their own method as per their area of interest:

  • General Navigation:
    1. Begin with high-level metrics for an overview.
    2. Utilize filters to pinpoint data relevant to specific departments, services, or timeframes.
  • Analysing Department and Service Performance:
    1. Select the desired department or service to evaluate their performance specifically.
    2. Identify departments/services with high overdue application rates to prioritize interventions.
  • Monitoring Service Delivery Speed:
    1. Analyse historical speed to identify patterns or delays in service delivery.
    2. Implement corrective actions in departments with slower service delivery.
  • Pendency Analysis:
    1. Quickly visualize current backlogs and aging applications.
    2. Address urgent backlogs (highlighted clearly in red/yellow).
    3. In case of Services which are only Integrated to Sewa Setu (with Service Delivery through dedicated application, such as Vahan-Sarthi or Basundhara) where the pendency pattern differs from the historical performance or internal reports, check if the status is correctly being transferred to Sewa Setu.
  • Trend Analysis:
    1. Observe monthly application trends to anticipate resource requirements, and to understand current pendency.
    2. Allocate resources efficiently based on identified demand peaks.

11. Intended Users:

  • Senior Stakeholders and Decision Makers: To oversee departmental efficiency, ensure accountability, and support strategic decision-making for enhanced public service delivery.
  • Officers/Institutions responsible for RTPS Compliance: To enable monitoring of compliance with the Service delivery commitments given in the RTPS Act.
  • Departmental Staff: To monitor service performance, manage application processing times, and address backlogs proactively.

This dashboard facilitates an informed, responsive, and accountable governance structure, significantly enhancing the effectiveness of public service delivery through Sewa Setu.

Known Issues

12. 12. The dashboard is only as good as the data that it surfaces. The present version is based on data currently available on the Sewa Setup portal at the end of March 2025. The data would be updated on a monthly basis by the first of the next month for timely monitoring by the stakeholders. Most of the services brought under the Sewa Setu portal to fulfil the vision of providing a Single Platform to the Citizens for all Government Services - “One Government, One Platform, Every Service” are provided through the dedicated application of the Departments, such as Basundhara or Vahan-Sarthi. Sewa Setu only serves as a bridge – not a destination, and hence it is essential that the link between Sewa Setu and the native applications are reliable and complete.

13. 13. The partial integration between Sewa Setu and the Service delivery applications cause data quality issues impact both accuracy and completeness. It is known that many of the links do not function correctly – such as that of Transport Department’s Vahan-Sarthi applications, leading to inaccurate display on the Dashboard. Some links are non-existent, such as the large number of services which are configured in the re-directional mode, where Sewa Setu only points the user to the relevant service, but is unable to track the status of processing of the application. There are also services where citizen’s apply both on Sewa Setu, and the native application, such as eDistrict, and where the service delivery status does not flow back to Sewa Setu.

14. 14. Over the next few months, the IT Department would work with service providing Departments to ensure that the link between Sewa Setup and the native application is made accurate and reliable. The Dashboard would also undergo improvements, based on the feedback received, to make it a single interface for monitoring all aspects of Service Delivery and compliance with the RTPS Act.
